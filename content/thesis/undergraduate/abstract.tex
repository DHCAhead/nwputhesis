% 中文摘要
\begin{abstract}
    边界表示（Boundary Representation, B-rep）是工业级几何建模的事实标准，但直接生成具有完整拓扑与曲面几何的 B-rep 仍是一个开放问题。近年来一些代表性工作分别从不同方向切入：DeepCAD 以草图—拉伸等参数化 CAD 操作序列为生成对象，SkexGen 进一步将草图与拉伸表征解耦并使用自回归 Transformer 在离散码本上采样，BrepGen 则在结构化潜空间上用扩散模型联合生成 B-rep 的几何与拓扑。这些方法在不同程度上依赖向量量化变分自编码器（VQ-VAE）或潜空间扩散，码本规模与潜变量分布的选择直接影响几何精度与拓扑闭合质量。

    本文围绕“不依赖显式 VQ-VAE 码本、直接对 B-rep 几何点序列进行自回归建模”这一思路，开展了点级 NURBS 序列化端到端 B-rep 生成方法的研究。主要工作包括以下三个方面：(1) 设计了一种面向 B-rep / NURBS 的点级量化与窗口化序列表示——对每个 NURBS 曲面在 UV 参数域上采样 16 个三维点并配 1 个面索引（face\_block = 49 个 token），对每条 NURBS 曲线按弧长采样 4 个三维点并配 2 个端面索引（edge\_block = 14 个 token），所有坐标分量经过 4000 级标量均匀量化后，与 50 个面索引和 3 个结构 token（START / SEP / END）共同构成 4053 维统一词表，PAD 在模型侧作为额外填充 token，使模型输出维度为 4054；(2) 实现了一种 LLaMA3 风格的标准 next-token 自回归生成模型，主干包含 RoPE、SwiGLU 与 RMSNorm 等组件，并提供 CompoundPointDecoder 作为代码中可选的窗口化分解扩展（本次主实验未启用，仅使用主干 norm 后接 ar\_lm\_head 的标准 next-token 头）；(3) 基于 OpenCASCADE 实现了从生成 token 序列到 NURBS 曲面拟合、拓扑缝合、STEP 文件导出以及 BRepCheck 校验的逆向重建与有效性评估闭环，给出了一条可独立运行的实验流水线。

    在 DeepCAD 子集（训练 64\,351 / 验证 6\,096）上以 4 张 NVIDIA H20 GPU 完整训练 200 个 epoch（端到端 17 小时 28 分钟），最终模型 hr2\_patch1 验证集交叉熵损失收敛至 0.10486。该模型在 1\,005 个独立采样并完整写出 STEP 文件的样本上经 BRepCheck 评测有效 980 个，有效率为 \textbf{97.51\%}，作为对照，早期未经 patch 化的 hr2 基线在 3\,000 个写出样本上的有效率为 90.40\%。该结果表明，在当前实验设定下，本文提出的点级序列化方案与标准 next-token 自回归生成路线初步可行，并满足毕业设计任务书提出的“有效率不低于 60\%”工程目标；但本文未进行跨数据集泛化测试，也未对序列化排序策略与 CompoundPointDecoder 的独立贡献进行受控对照实验，因此不对其单独贡献做定量声明，相关方向已在论文末尾作为后续工作列出。
    \keywordslist{B-rep, NURBS, 自回归 Transformer, LLaMA3, 点级序列化, OpenCASCADE}
\end{abstract}

% 英文摘要
\begin{engabstract}
    Boundary Representation (B-rep) is the de facto standard for industrial geometric modeling, yet generating B-rep models with complete topology and parametric surfaces directly with deep networks remains an open problem. Recent representative works approach this problem from different angles: DeepCAD generates parametric CAD operation sequences such as sketch-extrude; SkexGen further decouples the sketch and extrude representations and samples on disentangled discrete codebooks with an autoregressive Transformer; BrepGen models B-rep geometry and topology jointly with a diffusion model over a structured latent space. To different extents these methods rely on a VQ-VAE codebook or a latent diffusion prior, and the size of the codebook or the form of the latent distribution directly affects geometric precision and topological closure quality.

    Motivated by the idea of directly modeling B-rep geometry as an autoregressive point sequence without relying on an explicit VQ-VAE codebook, this thesis studies a point-level NURBS serialization framework for end-to-end B-rep generation. The work covers three aspects. (1) A point-level quantization and windowed serialization scheme is designed for B-rep / NURBS data. Each NURBS surface is sampled on its UV parameter domain into 16 three-dimensional points plus one face index (face block of 49 tokens), and each NURBS curve is sampled along arc length into 4 three-dimensional points plus 2 adjacent face indices (edge block of 14 tokens). Every coordinate component is uniformly quantized into 4000 levels, and together with 50 face indices and 3 structural tokens (START / SEP / END), they form a unified vocabulary of 4053 tokens; an additional PAD token is used inside the model so that the model output dimension is 4054. (2) A LLaMA3-style standard next-token autoregressive generative model is implemented, with RoPE, SwiGLU, and RMSNorm as core components. A CompoundPointDecoder is also available in the codebase as an optional window-decomposition extension, but is not activated in the main experiment of this thesis, which uses the standard backbone-norm-plus-ar\_lm\_head next-token head only. (3) An OpenCASCADE-based reverse-reconstruction pipeline is implemented to fit NURBS surfaces and curves from generated tokens, sew them into a Solid, export to STEP, and validate with BRepCheck, providing a self-contained pipeline for measuring generation validity.

    On a DeepCAD subset (64\,351 training / 6\,096 validation samples), the model is trained for 200 epochs with 4 NVIDIA H20 GPUs over 17 hours and 28 minutes. The final model, hr2\_patch1, reaches a validation cross-entropy of 0.10486. On 1\,005 sampled sequences for which a STEP file is successfully written, BRepCheck reports 980 as valid, yielding a valid rate of \textbf{97.51\%}. For reference, an earlier hr2 baseline (without the patch) attains a valid rate of 90.40\% on 3\,000 written samples. These figures provide preliminary evidence that the proposed point-level serialization and the standard next-token autoregressive pipeline are feasible under the current experimental setting and meet the 60\% valid-rate requirement of the undergraduate thesis brief. No cross-dataset generalization experiment has been performed, and no controlled ablation has been conducted to isolate the independent contribution of the topology-aware ordering or the CompoundPointDecoder; these directions are listed as future work at the end of the thesis.
    \engkeywordslist{B-rep, NURBS, autoregressive Transformer, LLaMA3, point-level serialization, OpenCASCADE}
\end{engabstract}
